# Volume 1 c001 Synopsis

## Purpose
- provide a chapter-level synopsis that can be used directly for prose drafting or further scene expansion
- preserve the current opening design: work-state first, anomaly embedded in labor routine, hard contradiction ending

## Chapter Synopsis

The chapter opens at the start of a maintenance shift in the mining belt, before anything openly extraordinary happens. The emphasis is on labor reality: stale ration taste still in the mouth, unfinished fatigue in the body, grimy tools, metallic condensation, and the heavy rhythm of people who work too close to pressure, steam, and collapse. The protagonist enters not as a chosen hero but as a low-tier technical worker whose value lies in noticing narrow, practical things other people are too rushed or too worn down to care about. His place in the hierarchy should be obvious immediately: he is useful, but replaceable; tolerated, but not central.

During ordinary inspection work, he encounters a small raw-value irregularity. It is not yet a dramatic alarm, and that is what makes it effective. It looks like the kind of thing a more complacent worker might explain away as instrument drift, residue noise, or routine instability. But the protagonist's way of reading the world is anchored in trace and pattern, not in what things are supposed to look like. He notices the anomaly, hesitates, and mentally marks it. The moment should show that he is already someone who lives with a quiet sense that the surface version of reality is not always enough.

That technical unease is then lightly echoed by bodily unease. A reflection in metal, gauge glass, or a thin pooled surface gives him a fractional mismatch: a hand that seems a half-beat out of step, a posture that does not feel fully synchronized, a glance that lands strangely. He does not interpret it as possession, revelation, or destiny. He suppresses it because he has likely learned that reacting to his own strangeness too openly is dangerous, embarrassing, or useless. The key is that technical wrongness and bodily wrongness arrive in the same chapter and begin to rhyme without being explained.

The chapter then pivots hard from routine into threat. A pressure-well instability or related mechanical escalation turns background danger into immediate danger. Sound, vibration, heat, shouted procedure, and chain-of-command pressure all narrow the available time to think. In that compressed moment, the protagonist has to decide what to trust: process, instruction, expected interpretation, or physical trace. This is where his first defining choice happens. He does not act like a reckless hero; he acts like someone who has learned to trust what the material world is telling him over what smoother authority would prefer be true.

He makes the risky choice based on raw state rather than expected workflow. The action helps stabilize or survive the emergency, but it does not resolve cleanly. The event should feel half like good judgment and half like something in him reacting too fast or too exactly for comfort. That overlap is important: his competence and his wrongness should brush against one another without collapsing into a full explanation.

The chapter ends not on recovery, but on contradiction. After the emergency, the protagonist has access to three versions of the event: what he physically did, what the raw values suggested, and what the system record or result says happened. Those three lines do not align. The mismatch must be concrete enough that neither he nor the reader can smooth it away as fear, confusion, or normal accident chaos. The closing effect of the chapter should be very clear: this is not only a dangerous industrial world, but a world in which logged truth may already be diverging from lived reality.

## Chapter Function Summary
- **World entry**: labor, fatigue, tools, hierarchy, atmosphere
- **Character entry**: narrow competence, guardedness, trace-trust
- **Mystery entry**: technical anomaly + bodily mismatch
- **Plot launch**: pressure-well emergency
- **Hook**: action / raw values / system record do not match

## Must Keep
- work-first opening, not lore-first opening
- protagonist's choice must feel specific to him
- bodily irregularity remains implicit
- end contradiction must be concrete and memorable

## Must Avoid
- early lore dump about factions, system history, or body truth
- explicit Black Falcon naming or co-speaking
- making the protagonist already understand the full nature of his abnormality
- ending the chapter on mood alone instead of hard contradiction
