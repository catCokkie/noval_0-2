# Volume 1 c001-c003 Scene Breakdown

## Scope
- scene-level planning for the opening three chapters only
- preserve current Volume 1 rules:
  - dominant contradiction = `real record vs official explanation`
  - Black Falcon remains implicit in volume 1 opening
  - protagonist knows he is "not normal" but does not understand why
- planning only; no prose drafting

## Global Guardrails
- every scene must do at least one of:
  - increase pressure
  - deepen world texture through lived detail
  - move the evidence chain
  - intensify bodily/internal irregularity without over-revealing it
- c001 must end on a hard contradiction
- c002 must end on active narrowing/rewrite, not vague suspicion
- c003 must end on systemic danger, not just personal concern

---

## c001 Scene Sequence

### Scene 1 - Shift Start / Work-State
- **Function**: establish labor reality, hierarchy, and protagonist's niche competence
- **Content**:
  - pre-shift routine, fatigue, ration or meal residue, work gear, and maintenance expectations
  - interactions that show the protagonist is low-tier but technically observant
  - slight hint that coworkers see him as a little odd or withdrawn
- **Pressure**: low, ordinary labor pressure
- **Reveal Limit**: no metaphysical framing

### Scene 2 - Routine Check / Minor Raw-Value Irregularity
- **Function**: place the first small anomaly inside normal work
- **Content**:
  - routine reading/check of equipment or well-state
  - one raw value feels off in a way that should be explainable
  - protagonist notices because he trusts trace and pattern more than surface reassurance
- **Pressure**: mild unease
- **Evidence Move**: first raw-value anomaly logged or mentally noted

### Scene 3 - Reflection / Bodily Mismatch
- **Function**: lightly braid bodily wrongness into technical wrongness
- **Content**:
  - metallic reflection, water surface, gauge glass, or polished housing creates a tiny mismatch
  - body timing / hand movement / gaze alignment feels fractionally wrong
  - protagonist suppresses the moment rather than interpreting it
- **Pressure**: internal unease rises
- **Reveal Limit**: no explicit second-self explanation

### Scene 4 - Pressure-Well Escalation
- **Function**: trigger the first real incident
- **Content**:
  - a pressure change, mechanical distress, or site warning turns the shift unstable
  - hierarchy and site procedure kick in
  - protagonist must decide whether to trust workflow, command, or physical evidence
- **Pressure**: emergency threshold
- **Evidence Move**: raw values and expected workflow diverge sharply

### Scene 5 - Distinctive Judgment / Emergency Action
- **Function**: make the protagonist choose like himself, not like a generic victim
- **Content**:
  - he chooses according to physical trace instead of pure process compliance
  - action succeeds partially, but not cleanly
  - bodily unease and tactical competence overlap without full explanation
- **Pressure**: life-risk plus accountability risk
- **Reveal Limit**: no confirmed internal voice or named agent

### Scene 6 - Hard Contradiction Ending
- **Function**: deliver the chapter hook
- **Content**:
  - after the emergency, the recorded/system result does not match what he did or what the equipment showed
  - the contradiction is concrete, not atmospheric
- **Pressure**: reality itself now unstable
- **End Requirement**: reader should feel the event cannot be smoothed into ordinary accident logic

## c002 Scene Sequence

### Scene 1 - Aftermath / Site Mood Shift
- **Function**: turn emergency into social pressure
- **Content**:
  - worker fear, gossip suppression, injury aftermath, sudden changes in tone and movement
  - authority presence or anticipation of authority presence
- **Pressure**: fear spreads laterally

### Scene 2 - Reporting Procedure
- **Function**: show how reality gets translated into paperwork
- **Content**:
  - protagonist must report, recount, sign, or verbally align with procedure
  - forms, phrases, and procedure language narrow what counts as truth
- **Pressure**: language itself becomes a trap
- **Evidence Move**: first comparison between experience and official formatting

### Scene 3 - Containment Language Arrives
- **Function**: introduce institutional vocabulary and cleanup instinct
- **Content**:
  - seal/safety or cleanup language reframes the incident
  - ordinary workers begin adapting themselves to the sanctioned version
- **Pressure**: social and institutional truth begin converging against lived truth
- **Reveal Limit**: no overt big-picture conspiracy dump

### Scene 4 - First Private Verification
- **Function**: make protagonist active
- **Content**:
  - he checks one thing privately: tool state, timing, residue, unofficial note, raw trace, or cached data
  - he finds enough to justify continued doubt, but not enough for open accusation
- **Pressure**: private truth becomes risky to hold
- **Evidence Move**: first hidden evidence line begins

### Scene 5 - End on Narrowing / Rewrite
- **Function**: close chapter on active distortion
- **Content**:
  - by chapter end, the official story is visibly shrinking or rewriting the event
  - protagonist realizes misunderstanding is too generous a word
- **Pressure**: truth is being managed in real time

## c003 Scene Sequence

### Scene 1 - Missing Witness Problem Appears
- **Function**: expand the incident beyond the original site
- **Content**:
  - a witness, coworker, or relevant low-tier figure cannot be found
  - absence is practical first, ominous second
- **Pressure**: evidence may disappear with people

### Scene 2 - Search Through Worker Spaces
- **Function**: widen the world through lived spaces
- **Content**:
  - dorms, meal lines, wash areas, tool lockers, rumor hubs, side alleys, ration windows
  - this is where labor-world texture deepens without leaving the main line
- **Pressure**: social silence and fear become visible

### Scene 3 - Record Erasure Clues
- **Function**: convert disappearance into structural danger
- **Content**:
  - signs that the witness is being erased from practical record chains
  - not just missing physically, but displaced in logs, assignments, belongings, or duty memory
- **Pressure**: system/institution can unmake a person administratively
- **Evidence Move**: disappearance linked to record manipulation

### Scene 4 - Attached Case Feeds Main Spine
- **Function**: prove attached cases serve the main contradiction
- **Content**:
  - a local detail from the search echoes the original accident: same residue, same delay, same language, same cleanup pattern
- **Pressure**: one event becomes repeatable structure
- **Reveal Limit**: no full explanation of who controls the structure

### Scene 5 - End on Systemic Risk
- **Function**: finish the golden-three-chapter lift
- **Content**:
  - protagonist understands that preserving the wrong truth can make a person disappear
  - his problem is no longer "what happened in one accident" but "what kind of machine handles truth this way"
- **Pressure**: continued attention now carries existential risk
- **End Requirement**: chapter must push the reader into c004 with stronger fear than c002 ended on
